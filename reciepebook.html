HOME MADE DOSA

ingredients:

  • 1 cup parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (black gram)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi seeds)
  • Water (for soaking and grinding)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Dosa batter
  • Oil or ghee (clarified butter) for greasing the pan

Instructions:

  1. Wash the rice and urad dal separately in water and soak them along with fenugreek seeds in enough water for about 6-8 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked rice and urad dal, and then grind them separately to a smooth paste using some water. You may need to do this in batches depending on the size of your grinder or blender.
  3. Mix the rice and urad dal paste together in a large bowl, add salt, and mix well. The consistency of the batter should be similar to pancake batter. Add water if needed to achieve the desired consistency.
  4. Cover the bowl with a clean cloth or plastic wrap and let the batter ferment for about 8-12 hours or overnight in a warm place. The batter should rise and become slightly airy.
  5. Once the batter has fermented, gently mix it again. If the batter is too thick, you can add a little water to adjust the consistency.
  6. Heat a flat non-stick or cast iron skillet (tava) over medium heat. Grease it lightly with oil or ghee.
  7. Pour a ladleful of the dosa batter onto the center of the skillet. Using the back of the ladle, spread the batter in a circular motion to form a thin, even layer. You can make the dosa as thin or thick as you prefer.
  8. Drizzle a few drops of oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa. Cook until the bottom becomes golden brown and crisp.
  9. If you're making a plain dosa, you're done! You can fold it and serve it with coconut chutney, sambar, or any chutney of your choice.
  10. If you're making a masala dosa, place a portion of masala potato filling on one side of the dosa. Fold the dosa over the filling to create a half-moon shape. Serve with chutney and sambar
  11. Repeat the process with the remaining batter, greasing the skillet lightly before making each dosa.

Dosas are traditionally served hot and crisp. Enjoy them with your favorite accompaniments!

Remember that making dosas might require a bit of practice to perfect the spreading technique, but the results are definitely worth it!

Pav Bhaji Recipe

Ingredients:

  1. 3 medium-sized potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed
  2. 1 cup mixed vegetables (such as peas, carrots, cauliflower, beans), boiled and finely chopped
  3. 1 large onion, finely chopped
  4. 1 large tomato, finely chopped
  5. 1/2 green bell pepper (capsicum), finely chopped
  6. 1/2 cup boiled and mashed pumpkin (optional)
  7. 1/2 cup boiled and mashed bottle gourd (optional)
  8. 4-5 cloves of garlic, minced
  9. 1-inch piece of ginger, grated or minced
  10. 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  11. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  12. 1-2 tablespoons pav bhaji masala (adjust to taste)
  13. 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  14. Salt, to taste
  15. Butter or oil, for cooking
  16. Fresh cilantro (coriander leaves), chopped, for garnishing

Instructions:

  • Heat butter or oil in a large pan or kadai over medium heat.
  • Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and chopped green chilies. Saut√© for a minute until fragrant.
  • Add chopped onions and cook until they turn translucent.
  • Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and pulpy.
  • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and pav bhaji masala. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes until the spices are well incorporated.
  • Add the finely chopped bell pepper and cook for a few minutes until it softens.
  • Add the boiled and mashed vegetables to the pan. Mix everything together.
  • Use a potato masher or the back of a spatula to mash and combine the vegetables, creating a thick and well-blended mixture.
  • Add salt to taste and adjust the spice levels if needed.
  • If the bhaji is too dry, you can add a little water to achieve the desired consistency. Let it simmer for a few more minutes.
  • Once the bhaji is cooked and well-mixed, remove it from the heat and garnish with chopped cilantro.

For the Pav:

  • Slit the pav rolls horizontally, leaving one side intact (like a hinge).
  • Heat a little butter on a griddle or pan and place the pav open side down. Lightly toast them until they turn golden and crispy.

To Serve:

  • Serve the hot and spicy bhaji with the toasted pav, lemon wedges, and onion slices.
  • You can also serve additional buttered pav on the side.
